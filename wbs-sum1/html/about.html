About Us

Let us introduce ourselves...

Wellington Botanical Society, established in 1939, has a membership of approximately 280 amateur and professional botanists. Our activities include research, field work, publication, advocacy, and the provision of botanical expertise. We support botanical research and endeavour through grants and advice to students.

Our Core Values

Conservation

We advocate for the conservation and protection of native New Zealand plants.

Knowledge

We aim to encourage the study of Botany, and fostering an interest in native plants and flora.

Cultivation

We aim to grow and protect rare New Zealand plants and flora.

People for plants

We are passionate about advocating the protection and preservaion of Aotearoas native plants. We also aim to encourage the study of botany, particularly New Zealand flora, fostering an interest in NZ native plants, especially in the field, encourage the cultivation of native plants under protected area statutes, of lands and waters in their natural state.

FAQ's

How often do you go on field trips?

Day trips to locations in the Wellington region are usually held on the first Saturday of each month. Extended excursions are usually held at New Year, Easter and the first weekends of February and December. Go to the 'Events' page for more information.

Do you have a location of business?

Currently, we do not have a business location. However we do meet up monthly for meetings where we discuss botany and plan other events, and have guest speakers. For more information on this, visit the 'Events' page.

How do I complete an award application?

Through our website, there are three types of awards that you can apply for. These awards are for people making great moves in the study of botany. To see more information on what awards we offer and how to apply for them, go to the 'Awards' page.

What do the different types of memberships mean?

Ordinary memberships are for anyone interested in the study of botany.
Student memberships are for people under the age of 18, or anyone who is a bona fide student.
Country memberships are for members living outside suburban bus and train services or as determined by the committee.
Group/Family memberships are for two people who are recieving a shared copy of newsletters and bullitens. Sign up here.

What perks do I get for signing up to be a member?

Not only does your membership support and contribute a great benefit to the study of Botany in Aotearoa, you are also advocating for the preservation of our native flora. You will recieve informative newsletters and biannual bullitens, interesting evening talks and field trips, and you can join us for a week long summer camp yearly. Sign up here.
